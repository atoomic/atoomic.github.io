# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

**Primary audience: hiring managers and recruiters** evaluating Nicolas R. for senior or leadership engineering roles. Their context is a 30-to-90-second scan — often on a second monitor or a phone — comparing him against other candidates. The job they are trying to get done is fast triage: *is this person credibly senior, technically deep, and current?* before deciding whether to open the résumé or reply.

The site also reaches technical peers (the Perl, AI, and open-source community) and prospective consulting and advisory clients through its open-source and experience sections, but the design optimizes for the hiring decision.

## Product Purpose

A single-page personal brand portfolio for Nicolas R. ("atoomic") that establishes him as a senior technology leader with rare depth in systems engineering — Perl, XS, CPAN authorship, memory profiling — who now leads agentic AI engineering. It exists to turn a brief visit into a résumé download and a conversation. Success is a hiring manager thinking *"this person is both deep and current, and clearly not generic"* within one screen, then downloading the résumé.

## Positioning

The rare engineer-leader who pairs deep systems internals (Perl/XS/CPAN) with modern agentic AI orchestration — able to move from C-level memory profiling to architecting LLM agent fleets, and to lead teams across both.

## Conversion & proof

- **Primary CTA:** Download the résumé. **Secondary:** send a message (contact) / connect on LinkedIn.
- **The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds:** *Deep systems engineer. Now orchestrating AI agents.*
- **Belief ladder** — what a hiring manager must believe, in order, before downloading:
  1. Nicolas is a credible senior technology leader (led 15-person teams; Director-level history at Weborama, cPanel, WebPros).
  2. He has rare, deep technical depth most leaders lack (Perl/XS internals, CPAN authorship, memory profiling, compiler work).
  3. He is current and forward-looking, not a legacy specialist (agentic orchestration, RAG, Claude/Anthropic SDK, AI-assisted development).
  4. He bridges the two — turning deep systems expertise into AI-driven delivery that scales teams.
  5. → Worth opening the résumé and starting a conversation.
- **Proof on hand:** the experience timeline and named CPAN modules (B::COW, Simple::Accessor, Clone, Slack::WebHook, grep.metacpan.org) already on the site. Testimonials/recommendations and quantified impact metrics are to be supplied — drop them in `.impeccable/assets/proof/` (text or screenshots) so they can be surfaced as evidence.

## Brand Personality

**Bold, vivid, and technically precise.** The site should feel like a confident poster, not a safe corporate page: high-energy, distinctive, willing to commit hard to its color so it is memorable in a sea of beige developer portfolios. The emotional goal is the *"how was this made?"* reaction — confidence, energy, and an unmistakable point of view — without ever slipping into the "hacker" costume. Swagger in the visuals; precision and credibility underneath.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / AI template** — indigo-to-violet gradient hero, glassy feature cards, a "trusted by" logo strip, and the big-number hero-metric template. The default AI-generated landing.
- **Stock "developer portfolio"** — terminal green-on-black, matrix rain, code snippets used as decoration. The cliché hacker costume.
- **Corporate beige / gray** — muted, safe, enterprise-drab neutrals that read as forgettable.
- **Overly minimal / empty** — too much whitespace, no imagery or texture; reads as unfinished rather than refined.

## Design Principles

- **Color is voice, not decoration.** The orange is a committed brand asset that owns surfaces and signals confidence; don't hedge it with neutral margins.
- **Show, don't tell.** Prove seniority and depth with concrete artifacts — timeline, modules, metrics, testimonials — never adjectives.
- **Make the bridge legible.** The systems-engineer-to-AI-orchestrator arc is the differentiator; the design surfaces it, it doesn't bury it.
- **Win the 10-second scan.** A hiring manager decides fast; the hero lands the positioning and makes the résumé download irresistible within one glance.
- **Engineered, not costumed.** Technical credibility through craft and precision — never the fake-terminal aesthetic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.2 AA**: body contrast ≥ 4.5:1, large text ≥ 3:1, full keyboard operability, visible focus indicators, `prefers-reduced-motion` honored, and screen-reader labels throughout. The existing baseline (skip link, ARIA roles, focus states, arrow-key section navigation, reduced-motion media query) is the starting point; the work is to audit and tighten it to AA consistently.
